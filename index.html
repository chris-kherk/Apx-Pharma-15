Risks and Side Effects

While steroid pills offer benefits, they also pose risks, especially when misused or abused:

Liver Toxicity

Liver toxicity from oral steroids occurs when these medications are taken by mouth, putting stress on the liver and potentially causing damage or problems with how the liver works. This happens because the liver processes substances like medications that enter the body through the digestive system. When steroids are taken orally, they travel through the stomach and into the liver, where they are processed. Over time, this process can strain the liver, leading to liver damage or dysfunction. It's important for people using oral steroids to be aware of this risk and to follow medical guidance to monitor and manage any potential liver concerns.

Cardiovascular Issues

Steroids, when used, can change cholesterol levels in your body. This means they can make your cholesterol levels go up or down, which might lead to a higher chance of having heart problems like heart disease or stroke. It's important to be cautious when using steroids and to talk to a doctor about any potential risks to your health.

Hormonal Imbalance

Extended use of these substances can interfere with the body's natural hormone production. This disruption can result in serious health issues such as infertility, impotence, or the development of breast tissue in men (known as gynecomastia). These effects occur because steroids can alter the balance of hormones in the body, causing unintended and sometimes irreversible changes. It's important for users to be aware of these risks and to consult healthcare professionals before starting any steroid regimen to understand potential consequences.

Psychological Effects

Some users of performance-enhancing substances might encounter mood swings, known as "roid rage", where emotions can quickly change from calm to intense anger. Some may also experience psychiatric disorders, which can affect their mental health and well-being. These effects can vary widely among individuals, and it's important for users to be aware of these potential risks and to consider consulting with a healthcare professional before starting or continuing steroid use.

Addiction and Dependence

Steroid abuse, when people use steroids in harmful ways, can lead to more than just physical problems. It can also cause psychological issues. This means that users might become mentally dependent on steroids, feeling like they need them to feel normal or strong.
